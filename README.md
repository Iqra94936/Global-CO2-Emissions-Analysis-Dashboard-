# Global-CO2-Emissions-Analysis-Dashboard-
This project explore the relationship between regional development and carbon footprints.
## Data Analysis Process
### Ask 
The project aims to answer three primary questions :
- Which reigons have seen the most significant growth in CO2 production?
- How does the total production compare to "Per Capita"(per person) average?
- Where are the current "hotspots" for emission globally?
  ### 2. Prepare and Process
  ** Data Sources: ** Google Data Analytics Course Resources
  ** Tools: **   Tableau
  ** Cleaning Steps :** Standardize country names
### 3. Analyze and share
  I created a dual-view dasnboard to compare raw output against individual footprints :
  ** Geospatial Map:** Uses Mapbox to visualize "Average CO2 Per Capita." This reveals that while some reigons
  produce more CO2, other much have higher individual footprints.
  ** Time Series Chart: ** Track CO2 production of region over 50 years.

## Key Insights
** The Great Shift ** The data clearly shows the ** East Asia & Pacific ** reigon(blue line) surpassing North America in total production
around the early 2000s, coinciding with rapid industrial expansion
** Per Capita Discrepancy :** High total production does not always means high per capita emissions.The map identifies specific clusters
in Europe and North America where per-capita emission remain significant higher than global average.
** Reigonal Trends :** While North America and Europe have shown slight decline in production growth, South Asia and East Asia
show exponential upward trends.
